# ATS Resume Audit Checklist

Use this before finalizing any optimized resume. Score each item 1 (weak) to 5 (excellent) or Yes/No. Aim for 85%+ overall pass rate and high keyword coverage.

## 1. Formatting & Parseability (Critical for Pass)
- [ ] Single-column layout with no tables, text boxes, sidebars, or complex elements
- [ ] Clean .docx or text-based PDF (test: can copy-paste all text cleanly into notepad?)
- [ ] Standard fonts (Arial/Calibri/etc. 10-12pt), consistent styling, no excessive bold/italics/underlines
- [ ] Standard section headings (Professional Summary, Skills, Professional Experience, Education, Certifications)
- [ ] Consistent date format (e.g., "Jan 2023 – Present") throughout
- [ ] 0.5–1" margins, appropriate white space (not cramped or sparse)
- [ ] No headers/footers with essential info; contact block at very top in plain text
- [ ] No photos, graphics, logos, icons, or emojis (unless explicitly creative role)
- [ ] Bullets are standard • or - ; no special characters that may break parsing
- [ ] Total length appropriate (1 page preferred, 2 max for senior)

## 2. Keyword & Semantic Alignment (Critical for Ranking)
- [ ] JD fully analyzed: must-have skills, preferred skills, responsibilities, tools, certifications extracted
- [ ] 70-90%+ of critical hard keywords appear naturally in resume (Summary + Skills + relevant bullets)
- [ ] Exact phrases from JD used in context (not just listed)
- [ ] Synonyms/variations included where natural (e.g., "Python" and "scripting in Python")
- [ ] Skills section explicitly lists high-value JD matches first
- [ ] Summary contextualizes keywords with your value/achievements
- [ ] No obvious keyword stuffing; language flows naturally and professionally
- [ ] Modern semantic fit: Bullets demonstrate the *meaning* behind JD requirements (impact, approach, tools)

## 3. Achievement Quality & Quantification (Key to Exceeding)
- [ ] Every bullet starts with strong, varied action verb (see action-verbs.md)
- [ ] Bullets follow Action + Context/Scope + Result (with metric) structure
- [ ] Metrics included wherever possible (%, $, time saved, scale, users impacted, rankings)
- [ ] Honest estimates/ranges used when exact numbers unavailable ("approximately 30%", "team of 8-12")
- [ ] Shows progression or increasing responsibility/impact over career
- [ ] Relevant to JD pain points or responsibilities (mirrors language where possible)
- [ ] No pure duty lists ("Responsible for X"); all transformed to impact

## 4. Structure & Prioritization
- [ ] Most relevant/recent experience and achievements appear first and get most detail
- [ ] Professional Summary is tailored, specific, and compelling (3-5 lines max)
- [ ] Skills section is prominent, categorized if helpful, and JD-aligned
- [ ] Irrelevant or outdated experience condensed or removed
- [ ] Education/Certs clear, with dates and issuing bodies; extras only if value-adding
- [ ] Optional sections (Projects, Volunteer, Publications) only if they reinforce JD keywords or story

## 5. Overall Impact & Story
- [ ] Resume tells a clear, scannable story of "This person is an excellent fit because..."
- [ ] Top half (Summary + first 1-2 roles) contains strongest JD alignment and proof
- [ ] Language is confident, active, and professional (no "I", minimal "we" unless team achievement)
- [ ] Free of typos, inconsistencies, or vague claims
- [ ] Would pass 6-10 second recruiter skim test with key points jumping out
- [ ] For target role level: Appropriate seniority tone (strategic for senior, execution + impact for mid)

## Quick Scoring Guide
- **90%+**: Excellent – high chance of parsing cleanly + ranking well. Submit with confidence.
- **75-89%**: Good – minor tweaks needed (usually more metrics or better keyword weaving).
- **<75%**: Needs significant work – focus on formatting first, then content tailoring.
- **Keyword Coverage Tool Tip**: Paste JD + resume into Jobscan.co, TealHQ, or similar for automated % match (target 80%+ on must-haves).

Print or copy this checklist for each optimization project. Re-audit after major edits.
